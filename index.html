Product History and Market

History

The idea that energy, power, or electricity could be transferred between two points has been around since at least 1831. At the time, it was Michael Faraday leading the charge in this area, demonstrating that magnetic induction can occur when you have an oscillating electrical current in one coil of wire sitting close to another. Then, Nikola Tesla started to dream up his next invention: the transfer of electrical current, and ultimately power, without the need or use of wires.

Bonus: How does it work?

Operation of the charger

Wireless chargers use magnetism to transmit energy. They use a loop of coiled wired around a bar magnet, which is known as an inductor. When electric current passes through the coiled wire, it creates an electromagnetic field around the magnet. This field can be used to transfer voltage to a receiver nearby. The magnetic field creates current in the coil inside the device sitting on the wireless device, then the magnetic energy is converted to electrical energy which is used to charge the battery.
